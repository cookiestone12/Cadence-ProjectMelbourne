# 1. About Cadence

## Headline

**Treat your catalog like the financial asset it is.**

## Subheadline

Cadence is the operating system for modern music catalogs — built for labels, publishers, managers, and rights holders who are tired of running a multi-million-dollar business on spreadsheets.

## The Problem We Solve

The music industry generates billions of dollars a year in recorded music and publishing revenue, yet the people who own those catalogs still manage them with tools built for a different era. Royalty statements arrive as 500-page PDFs. Splits live in email threads. Schedule A documents take weeks to assemble. Catalog valuations are guesswork. And when it’s time to sell, refinance, or audit a catalog, the data is scattered across a dozen systems that were never designed to talk to each other.

Cadence exists to change that.

## What Cadence Does

Cadence is a multi-tenant SaaS platform that brings catalog management, rights intelligence, royalty processing, and valuation into a single system. We built it around one idea: every song in your catalog is a financial asset, and it deserves to be tracked, analyzed, and valued like one.

With Cadence, you can:

- **Manage your catalog end-to-end** — from unreleased demos to fully commercialized recordings, with automated release triggers and date-stamped edit history for every work.
- **Process royalty statements intelligently** — upload statements from BMI, ASCAP, the MLC, distributors, and sub-publishers, and let the royalty engine normalize line items down to the individual song and client.
- **Value your catalog with real data** — combine years of statement history with streaming consumption data to generate defensible catalog valuations.
- **Audit your earnings** — cross-reference what you were paid against what you should have been paid, and catch missing streams before they disappear.
- **Pitch for sync with AI** — match your catalog to sync briefs automatically using metadata-driven search.
- **Give clients their own portal** — let the creators you represent log in, review their catalog, upload contracts, and see their earnings without a phone call.

## Who We Built It For

Cadence serves independent labels, music publishers, production companies, artist managers, catalog investors, and the administrators who support them. Whether you manage ten songs or ten thousand, the platform scales with you.

## Our Philosophy

Software in the music industry is too often designed for accountants or engineers, not for the creative people whose livelihoods depend on it. Cadence is built on three principles:

1. **Simplicity over jargon.** If a kindergartener couldn’t figure out the core workflow, we haven’t finished designing it.
1. **The catalog is the asset.** Every feature should help you understand, grow, or defend the value of your catalog.
1. **Your data is yours.** We give you the tools, but you own everything you put into the platform. Export at any time. No lock-in.

## Where We Are

Cadence Catalog Intelligence Co. is a Delaware C-Corporation headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, with team members across the United States and the Caribbean.

## Get In Touch

To request a demo, partner with us, or learn more about Cadence, reach out at **communication@cadence-ci.com**.

# 4. Anti-Fraud Policy

**Last Updated: [DATE]**

Cadence Catalog Intelligence Co. (“Cadence”) is committed to maintaining the integrity of the music rights ecosystem. This Anti-Fraud Policy explains what we consider fraudulent activity on the Cadence platform and how we respond.

## 1. Prohibited Activity

The following activities are strictly prohibited on Cadence and will result in immediate account suspension or termination, forfeiture of fees, and referral to law enforcement or relevant industry authorities:

**Ownership and Rights Fraud**

- Claiming ownership of songs, recordings, or compositions you do not actually own or control
- Submitting false or forged contracts, split sheets, or Schedule A documents
- Misrepresenting the percentage of ownership you hold in a work
- Registering works under another person’s IPI, CAE number, or publisher account without authorization

**Royalty and Statement Fraud**

- Uploading fabricated or altered royalty statements
- Manipulating royalty data to inflate earnings, valuations, or audit results
- Filing false audit claims against performing rights organizations, publishers, or distributors based on manipulated Cadence reports
- Attempting to double-collect royalties on the same work through multiple accounts

**Identity and Account Fraud**

- Creating an account using false information
- Impersonating another person, artist, or business entity
- Using stolen payment methods
- Creating multiple accounts to evade suspension or abuse free-tier limits
- Unauthorized access to another user’s account

**Platform Abuse**

- Using Cadence to facilitate money laundering, tax evasion, or other financial crimes
- Reverse engineering the platform to extract data belonging to other users
- Automated scraping of catalog, contact, or royalty data

## 2. How We Detect Fraud

Cadence uses a combination of automated monitoring and manual review to detect suspicious activity, including:

- Automated flagging of unusual upload patterns, duplicate registrations, and conflicting ownership claims
- Cross-referencing of catalog data against publicly available sources
- Manual review of accounts that trigger risk signals
- User reports submitted through the platform

## 3. What Happens When Fraud Is Detected

If we identify suspected fraudulent activity, we may take one or more of the following actions without prior notice:

- Suspend or terminate the account
- Freeze catalog data and royalty processing
- Notify affected parties (including other Cadence users whose rights may be implicated)
- Report the activity to relevant performing rights organizations, publishers, or distributors
- Refer the matter to law enforcement
- Pursue civil remedies, including recovery of damages and legal fees

You agree that Cadence has no liability for any losses you incur as a result of enforcement actions taken under this policy.

## 4. Reporting Fraud

If you believe another user is engaged in fraudulent activity on Cadence, or if your rights have been misrepresented on the platform, report it to **communication@cadence-ci.com**. Please include:

- The name of the user or organization you are reporting
- A description of the suspected fraudulent activity
- Supporting documentation (contracts, registration records, statements)
- Your contact information

All reports are reviewed confidentially. We do not disclose the identity of the reporter to the reported party unless required by law.

## 5. Cooperation With Authorities

Cadence cooperates fully with law enforcement, performing rights organizations, and industry bodies investigating fraud. We may share user information and platform data in response to valid legal process or credible fraud investigations.

## 6. Contact

Report suspected fraud to **communication@cadence-ci.com**.

## Catalog Management

**How do I add songs to my catalog?**
You have several options:

1. **Manually** — create a song entry one at a time from the Catalog page
1. **Spotify import** — paste a Spotify playlist or album link to pull songs with metadata already attached
1. **Schedule A upload** — upload a CSV, Excel, PDF, or Word document and let our AI map the columns for you
1. **Dropbox or Google Drive scan** — link a folder and let Cadence scan for audio files and match them to your catalog

**What’s the difference between Released and Unreleased works?**
A Released work has an official release date that has already passed. An Unreleased work is anything else — demos, placed-but-not-yet-released songs, sync pitches, instrumentals. Cadence automatically moves an Unreleased work to Released on its release date if you’ve set one.

**How do splits work in Cadence?**
Splits are entered at the client level. When you create a song, you’ll assign a percentage to each contributing writer or producer. If an organization owns a share in the song, create a separate client entry for that organization and assign splits accordingly. Changes to splits are date-stamped in the song’s edit history.

**Can I track a song’s edit history?**
Yes. Every change to a song — splits, contributors, metadata, release status — is logged in the song’s edit history with a timestamp and the user who made the change. This creates a verifiable digital trail of how the song evolved.

**How do I handle remixes, instrumentals, or alternate versions?**
Use the Duplicate feature on the song detail page. It creates a new entry linked to the original, so you can adjust splits and metadata without re-entering everything.

## Royalty Processing

**How do I upload a royalty statement?**
Go to Royalties → Upload Statement. Select the source (BMI, ASCAP, MLC, distributor, etc.), upload the file, and Cadence will parse it, map columns, and match line items to songs in your catalog.

**What file formats are supported?**
CSV, Excel (XLSX), and PDF. Our AI handles most formats from major performing rights organizations and distributors. If we don’t recognize your source, contact support and we’ll add it.

**What happens if a line item doesn’t match a song?**
Unmatched transactions appear in the Unmatched queue. You can manually match them to a song, create a new song entry, or dismiss them. Cadence learns from your matches over time.

**How does Cadence handle MLC statements with multiple clients?**
MLC statements often contain line items that apply to multiple clients you manage. Cadence detects MLC statements and automatically splits line items across the relevant clients based on their mirrored splits.

**Can I see how much each song is earning?**
Yes. Open any song to see its earnings history, revenue trends, and decay curve based on the statements you’ve uploaded.

## Valuations

**How does catalog valuation work in Cadence?**
Upload several years of royalty statements and Cadence calculates a baseline valuation using historical earnings, decay analysis, and (if enabled) Luminate consumption data. You can apply multiples and export the valuation report.

**What data do I need for an accurate valuation?**
Ideally three years of royalty statements across all your income sources. The more complete your data, the more defensible the valuation.

**What is the Luminate integration?**
Luminate provides streaming consumption data. When integrated, Cadence cross-references your catalog against Luminate’s numbers to give you a more accurate picture of what your catalog is actually generating — and whether you’re being paid for all of it. Available on the Enterprise + Luminate tier.

Here’s the deal. The DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act, 17 U.S.C. § 512) gives online platforms “safe harbor” protection from copyright infringement claims when users upload content — but only if the platform meets a specific set of requirements. The biggest one is that we have to formally designate a DMCA agent and register that agent with the U.S. Copyright Office through their Designated Agent Directory at dmca.copyright.gov. It’s a public filing, it costs about $6, and it has to be renewed every three years. Until we actually do that filing, publishing a DMCA policy on the site would be misleading and wouldn’t give us any legal protection.

On top of the registration, we need to make a few decisions as a team:

1. Who is the designated agent? It can be a person (one of the founders) or a role (“DMCA Agent, Cadence Catalog Intelligence Co.”). Most early-stage startups just name a founder.
1. What physical address do we list? It has to be a real mailing address — usually the registered agent address from our Delaware incorporation, or our Atlanta operating address.
1. Who monitors the inbox? DMCA notices have legal deadlines attached (we have to act “expeditiously” on valid takedown notices), so whoever owns communication@cadence-ci.com needs to know how to triage a DMCA claim when one comes in.
1. Do we want a separate dmca@ address, or keep everything routed through communication@? For launch I’m fine keeping it consolidated, but once we have real users uploading catalog data we should probably split it out.
