Cadence CI — 40-Minute Product Demo
Opening (3 min)
Introduce the problem: music catalogs are financial assets being managed with spreadsheets, scattered PDFs, and guesswork. Cadence treats your catalog the way a portfolio manager treats a fund.
Module 1: Catalog Onboarding & Ingestion (5 min)
Walk through uploading a catalog — importing royalty statements (PDF parser), connecting distributor/PRO data, and showing how Cadence normalizes everything into a single unified view. Highlight how messy publisher PDFs get structured automatically.
Module 2: Catalog Dashboard & Intelligence (5 min)
Show the main dashboard — total catalog value, revenue trends, top-performing assets, genre/writer splits. This is the “command center” moment where the user sees their catalog as a living portfolio for the first time.
Module 3: Royalty Accounting & Reconciliation (7 min)
This is the deepest module — walk through how royalties flow in from multiple sources, how Cadence matches and reconciles across publishers, distributors, and PROs. Show split management, payment tracking, and the recoupment engine in action. Flag discrepancies the platform catches automatically.
Module 4: Rights Intelligence (5 min)
Show ownership mapping — who owns what, where, and for how long. Demonstrate how Cadence tracks reversion dates, territorial rights, and admin windows. This is where catalog owners and managers realize they’ve been flying blind.
Module 5: Catalog Valuation & Analytics (7 min)
The showpiece. Walk through valuation modeling — NPS multiplier, decay analysis, half-life modeling, HHI concentration scoring. Show how a user can generate an investor-ready valuation snapshot. Reference the kind of data room work you’ve done for clients like Cheeze Beatz — this is that, automated.
Module 6: Data Room & Export (3 min)
Show how everything packages into a shareable data room for investors, acquirers, or legal. One-click export of valuation reports, ownership summaries, and royalty histories.
Closing & Pricing (5 min)
Recap the tiers — Free for basic catalog view, Standard ($29/mo) for indie writers, Professional ($99/mo) for managers and small labels, Enterprise ($499/mo) for serious catalog operations, Enterprise + Luminate ($699/mo) for institutional-grade intelligence. Leave time for questions.

Pacing notes:
	∙	Modules 3 and 5 get the most time because they carry the most “aha” moments and differentiation
	∙	Keep Module 1 tight — onboarding is important but not exciting in a demo
	∙	If running short, compress Module 6 into the closing
	∙	If you’re demoing to investors vs. end users, shift weight toward Module 5 and the data room; for operators, lean into Module 3
